\textbf{Revenue} is defined as all money a business generates through the sale of goods or 
services, before expenses are considered. There are two types of revenue: \textbf{gross 
revenue}, calculated by multiplying total units sold by the unit selling price, and 
\textbf{net revenue}, which is gross revenue less any purchase discounts and 
returns.\footcite{revenue}

\textbf{Profit} arises when revenue exceeds expenses. There are three measures of 
profit\footcite{kenton_2024_understanding}:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Gross Profit} = Revenue $-$ Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
    \item \textbf{Operating Profit} = Gross Profit $-$ Operating Expenses $-$ 
    Depreciation \& Amortization (D\&A), where depreciation applies to tangible assets 
    and amortization to intangible assets such as patents and licences
    \item \textbf{Net Profit} (``the bottom line'') = Earnings Before Interest and Tax 
    (EBIT) $-$ Interest Expense $-$ Tax
\end{itemize}

Companies may distribute profits to shareholders as dividends, or reinvest them to fund future growth.

\textbf{Profitability} measures the efficiency with which a company generates profit relative to its size or resources. Key profitability ratios include\footcite{horton_2026_understanding}:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Gross Profit Margin} = Gross Profit $\div$ Total Revenue
    \item \textbf{Net Profit Margin} = Net Profit $\div$ Total Revenue
    \item \textbf{Return on Assets (ROA)} = Net Income $\div$ Total Assets
    \item \textbf{Return on Average Assets (ROAA)} = Net Income $\div$ Average Total Assets
    \item \textbf{Return on Equity (ROE)} = Net Income $\div$ Shareholders' Equity
\end{itemize}

 Key factors influencing profitability include expenses, demand, productivity, and competition.\footcite{horton_2026_understanding}
